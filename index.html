Elephant

An elephant is the largest land animal, known for its massive size, long trunk, large ears, and tusks (in some species). There are three main species: the African bush elephant, African forest elephant, and Asian elephant. Elephants play a crucial role in ecosystems but face threats from habitat loss and poaching.

hippopotamus

A hippopotamus (or hippo) is a large, semi-aquatic mammal native to sub-Saharan Africa. Known for its barrel-shaped body, massive mouth, and nearly hairless skin, it spends most of its time in rivers and lakes to stay cool. Despite their bulky look, hippos are fast runners and strong swimmers!

Rhinoceros

A rhinoceros (or rhino) is a large, thick-skinned herbivore known for its distinctive horn (made of keratin). There are five species rhinoceros: white rhinoceros, black , Indian , Javan rhinoceros, and Sumatran rhinos Rhinos play a vital role in their ecosystems but need urgent conservation efforts.
